Sarah Chen · Today 10:00 AM · AI Generated

Background Summary

Sarah is a data scientist at a mid-size fintech company in San Francisco (4 years). She has a Master's in Statistics from Stanford and strong programming skills in Python and R. She became interested in AI safety after reading "The Alignment Problem" and attending an AI safety reading group. She has no formal background in ML research but has experience with applied ML (fraud detection, risk modeling). She's considering a significant career change and is willing to invest in upskilling.

Goals & Motivations

Sarah wants to transition from applied data science to AI safety technical research. She's motivated by a genuine concern about existential risk and feels her current work isn't contributing meaningfully. Her ideal outcome is a role at an alignment research organization where she can contribute technically. She's also open to AI safety-adjacent roles (governance, policy) if the technical path proves too difficult given her background.

Key Constraints

  • Financial: Has ~$40K in savings, could handle 6 months without income but not much more
  • Geographic: Strongly prefers Bay Area (partner's job is there)
  • Visa: US citizen, no restrictions
  • Timeline: Wants to make the transition within 12 months
  • Skills gap: Lacks formal ML research experience and publications

Suggested Talking Points

  1. Bridge the skills gap: Explore whether the MATS program or ARENA would be a good fit to build alignment-specific research skills while she's still employed
  2. Leverage existing strengths: Her statistics background and applied ML experience are more relevant to alignment than she might think - discuss how interpretability and evaluation work draws on these skills
  3. Realistic timeline: 12 months is tight for a full career pivot into research. Discuss whether a stepping-stone role (e.g., research engineer vs. researcher) might be more achievable
  4. Financial planning: If she does MATS or similar, that's ~3 months unpaid. Discuss whether her savings can support this while maintaining Bay Area living costs
  5. Network building: She's relatively new to the AI safety community. Suggest specific meetups, conferences, and online communities
  6. Alternative paths: If direct research feels too far, explore AI safety-adjacent roles at Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind (policy, safety evaluations, red teaming) that she could get with her current background
  7. Publication strategy: Discuss whether writing a short alignment-relevant blog post or paper could help signal commitment and build credibility

Relevant Resources

  • MATS (ML Alignment Theory Scholars) - Mentored research program, ideal for her transition. Next cohort applications open March 2026.
  • ARENA (Alignment Research Engineer Accelerator) - More technical, could fill her ML research gaps.
  • AI Safety Fundamentals Course - Good starting point if she hasn't done structured alignment learning yet.
  • 80,000 Hours AI Safety Career Guide - Comprehensive overview of the landscape she's entering.
  • Alignment Forum - For engaging with ongoing technical discussions and building visibility.

Potential Connections

  • Dr. Nina Patel (MIRI) - Can speak to what alignment research looks like day-to-day and what backgrounds are valued.
  • Sam Whitfield (Anthropic) - Could discuss policy research roles as an alternative entry point. (Note: busy until April)

Watch Out For

  • She may underestimate how transferable her statistics skills are - help her see the value in her existing expertise
  • Financial constraints could create pressure to rush the transition. Encourage thoughtful planning over speed
  • Her openness to policy/governance as a "backup" may indicate less clarity about what she actually wants - probe this
